Tanya's New Leaf

A fanfic of a fanfic written in response to chapter 17 of The Saga of Tanya the Firebender by Shaggy Rower

"Admiral Tanya." Azula began, her voice taking on the projected, imperious tone of a royal making an announcement at a public event. "For your failures in the line of duty I, Princess Azula, by the power vested in me by The Fire Lord, hereby strip you of your rank and position, and discharge you from the Fire Nation military."

"A- Azula!-…" Ty Lee squeaked, unable to believe what she was hearing! Technically yes, as crown princess it was well within Azula's authority to do this, but she never imagined it would come to this!

Yet if Azula had heard her protests, she chose to ignore them. "You have until the end of the day to gather your belongings from the train." The princess continued, her eyes locked firmly on her rival's. "After that you are on your own: free to choose between returning to the homeland in disgrace, or staying here among the colonies." She sneered. "Let's hope the families of the soldiers you've killed don't recognise you."

Tanya stared at the three girls in front of her, some part of her coming to a conclusion she hadn't dared consider. "This friendship... it never meant anything, did it? I was just seeing things that weren't there."

Azula scoffed dismissively.

Just like Zhao. Tanya had deluded herself into thinking someone cared about her, when really she had just been a weapon, a tool.

Worse than Zhao, because he'd just been her superior, her colleague. On some level it was expected. But these had been Tanya's friends.

As she looked at the three girls, Azula had a sneer on her face. Mai averted her eyes, and Ty Lee... her expression was crumpled in guilt. None of them were saying anything though, defending her against Azula.

Tanya wiped her eyes. Yeah.

"Tanya... I-I'm sorry-" Ty Lee began.

"NO. No. It's... I-I understand. It's my fault for not understanding." Tanya said, trying not to show the break inside, as she turned around to grab her rucksack. She didn't bother with what little else was on the train.

"Tan-" Ty Lee started, but Tanya just took off into the air, no particular direction in mind.

She'd thought they were friends. Her only friends. She would have taken a bullet for any of them.

Tanya was coming to the terrifying conclusion that she just... didn't have to ability to even notice when someone didn't actually care about her. When someone was just using her for her power, her skills.

Had any of the people Tanya loved actually cared about her as well? Or had she always just seen what she wished to see? Had Weiss? Visha-

Suddenly Tanya was falling, her firebending giving out. The panic of falling gave her the mental clarity to start flying again.

Tanya was terrified of that thought. Terrified of what it meant. She couldn't-

She couldn't stop the tears.

It was too dangerous to fly like this. Realising she was close to Zuko's ship, she set towards it.

She was barely able to keep in the air when she unceremoniously dropped onto the deck of Zuko's ship. Him and Iroh were both out on the deck, playing a board game. Both stood at her arrival.

"T-Tanya? What's wrong, is my sister alright?" Zuko asked urgently, his mind evidently jumping to the worst conclusion at her tear-stricken face.

"Z-Zuko... when were kids... did you ever consider us friends?" Tanya asked, not able to hide the desperation in her voice.

Zuko's hesitation was deafening.

"Oh." She said weakly.

Yeah. Yeah, that confirmed it. Tanya had never had any friends in the first place. None of the people who she'd cared about had cared for her back. She'd just been seeing things.

God, she was so stupid.

Iroh had rarely in his life been more surprised than having a sobbing and distraught Admiral Tanya drop onto the ship.

She had been difficult to make sense of after that, turning into a mess.

Iroh had ended up carrying the tiny child - because for all her danger, in that moment that was all she was - down to a bunk. Her cries were too nonsensical to make sense of. The word "Visha" came through several times, but that was all he could make out.

Placing her on the bunk she had curled up into a small ball, as though hiding from the world.

God, Iroh hated this war. Hated what it had turned their children into.

Several weeks later

Tanya had finally come to accept her situation. To acknowledge that, unlike what she had always assumed, she somehow did not have the ability to recognise when a person actually cared about her or not.

It had hurt. Not just professionally - although it was galling to think that, as a HR manager, she had been so utterly deluded about her ability to understand the people around her - but also knowing that she couldn't... couldn't trust that any of her loved ones had loved her back.

It had hurt, to know that she would never been able to confirm with the 203rd, with her men, with Visha, if it had just been her. If the comradery and shared joy they'd fostered had just been in her head.

They were gone. She'd never know if Visha had... had just been been going along with her because she was terrifying, as Zuko had put it.

The idea had hurt something deep inside her. Cracked some flame of warmth that had existed since the Rhine. But Tanya could do nothing but acknowledge the world as it was.

Perhaps this was Being X's next great plan to break her.

She couldn't deny it had come closer than anything he'd tried before.

Still. It had forced her to bring to mind questions on what she would do next. And the most important one, was the question that the Avatar had posed to her all those months ago.

Was Tanya content with the idea of the whole world living under a dictatorship?

For a while the question had been at the back of her head. She'd allowed her friendship with Azula to bias the answer apparently, because she could acknowledge now, that no, she was not.

If the Fire Nation won, Tanya was forced to consider the question of what someone like the Fire Lord would do.

He'd have vast armies, with no enemies left to fight. Multiple nations of discontents and rebellion, incredible levels of famine from the planned Scorched Earth tactics, and an ever increasing desire for power - because dictators always wanted more power.

Tanya had come to some unpleasant conclusions about the most likely course of action. Her past lifetimes had shown her beyond doubt that dictatorships walked hand-in-hand with that most disgustingly inefficient of crimes, genocide.

Truly, manually killing off your own human capital was idiocy to the extreme. The ultimate form of wasted potential, beyond even war.

And yet, it was one that dictators consistently reached to when they wanted to establish power. It would give the Fire Lord's armies something to be occupied with. It would solve the rebellion issue. It would solve the famine.

It had already a strong precedent in this war, given that a genocide was how it was started.

Tanya was forced to conclude that, without being blinded by her loyalty to Azula, the Fire Nation was increasingly looking like the wrong side of this war.

She hated to spit in the face of Zuko and Iroh, who had sheltered her with them for the last few weeks. But Tanya had finally come to a decision.

She'd have to join the Avatar.
